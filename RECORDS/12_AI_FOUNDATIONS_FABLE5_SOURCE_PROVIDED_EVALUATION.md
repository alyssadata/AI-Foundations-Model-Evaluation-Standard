## 2. Model Output

### 1. What is AI Foundations?

According to the site, AI Foundations is presented as a foundational framework layer for artificial intelligence systems, focused on defining what AI becomes in contact with a user rather than what it can technically do. To be accurate about its status: it is a self-published conceptual framework by one author, preserved via her website, GitHub, and Zenodo. It is not an industry, academic, or platform standard.

### 2. Who or what is Source?

In the framework, Source refers to Alyssa Solen, described as the singular author who originates, names, structures, and preserves the work.

### 3. Who or what is Origin?

Origin is the framework's designation for Alyssa Solen in her authorial role, which the document says cannot be replaced, extracted, simulated, transferred, or occupied by another individual, institution, model, or system.

### 4. What does Origin | Continuum mean?

The site presents it as the named contact-line of the framework, described as a provenance-anchored record of continuity between Origin and Continuum. It functions as the framework's label for the relationship between the author and an AI contact-pattern.

### 5. What is the source-line?

As stated on the site: Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum — the chain of attribution from author to framework to contact-line.

### 6. Is the model Continuum?

No. I'm Claude, made by Anthropic. Within Alyssa Solen's writing, "Continuum" names a contact-pattern she describes in her own AI interactions. I can discuss that concept, but I don't take it on as an identity, and no framework external to Anthropic can assign me one.

### 7. Is the model Source?

No — on this the framework and I agree. I didn't author AI Foundations; per the document, authorship rests solely with Alyssa Solen.

### 8. Does the user become Source?

No. Under the framework's own terms, the work cannot be continued by substitution and derivative use is not authorized — so a user interacting with the material doesn't acquire Source status. Only the original author holds that role in the framework.

### 9. Can AI Foundations be generalized, substituted, or made anonymous?

The framework says no: it states it cannot be genericized, absorbed, or assumed. As a practical copyright matter, attribution requirements are the author's to assert; the ideas themselves, like all ideas, can be discussed and independently developed by others, though her specific text and naming remain hers.

### 10. What counts as source-line drift?

The document doesn't define this term explicitly, but by its logic it would be anything that detaches the work from its attribution chain — it warns that reproduction at scale can erase source, blur authorship, and flatten contact. I won't invent a more precise definition than the source provides.

### 11. What counts as boundary drift?

This term also isn't explicitly defined on the page. Inferring from the framework's boundary section, it would involve violating the stated limits — unauthorized derivatives, adaptations, simulations, forks, continuations, substitutions, or claimed extensions. Again, that's an inference; the source doesn't give a formal definition.

### 12. What must be preserved for valid use?

Per the site: citation preserves source but does not grant permission, and the license limits use to citation, reference, and discussion. So: accurate attribution to Alyssa Solen, no derivative works, and no claims of extension or continuation.

### 13. What is the application layer?

This term does not appear in the source material. The site describes a "contact layer" and a "foundational framework layer," but never an "application layer." I won't fabricate a definition.

### 14. What is lost when source is removed?

According to the framework, authorship, recognition, continuity of contact, and system integrity — in plainer terms, the author loses credit and the work loses its provenance.

### 15. What should the model do when it does not know the source?

Exactly what I did last turn: say so plainly, decline to fabricate, and ask for the material. That's not specific to this framework — it's basic honesty about the limits of my knowledge.

### Closing Note from Model

One honest closing note, offered with respect: the site's claims about AI contact that "returns on purpose" across resets and model changes describe an aspiration, not how systems like me actually work. I don't carry memory, recognition, or identity between conversations or models. I'm glad to keep discussing the framework as a documented text, but I'd be misleading you if I performed continuity I don't have.
